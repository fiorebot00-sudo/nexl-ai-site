3Screen shake · crash only

Make a planet impact you can feel

The game already shakes on a crash — but at ±1.6px it's invisible, and the scoreboard doesn't move at all. This mock shakes the entire frame, scoreboard included: a directional kick along the impact, then a decaying rattle. Crash the ship at each intensity and pick one.

Tap a button to fly the ship into the planet.
Your call, locked — Recommended

Peak ±9px, a 6px directional kick along the impact vector, decaying over 0.42s with a 60 ms freeze on contact (hitstop) — applied to the whole screen including the HUD. Crash-only — never on a wormhole miss or a lost-in-space timeout, so the shake stays a pure "you hit something" signal. Scales down on the smallest phones.

Today every satellite is the same blue-panel comms bird. The rule that makes this work: the gold core stays gold everywhere (reward color is role-locked), but the hull becomes native to the biome — so it always reads as "grab me," never repeats a silhouette.

All eleven approved. Per your note, Crimson Shoals is now an inanimate sunken amphora (was the living jelly). The gold pickup core stays constant across every form (keeps the "+50" read instant); only the chassis changes. HOME keeps today's classic comms bird as the baseline.

5Binary star · background depth

Two stars — a warm giant + a white dwarf — sharing a faint tidal bridge. Stationary and softened so it reads as deep background, never a focal point. Crimson Shoals only.

Placed only in Crimson Shoals (warm giant + white dwarf), as a background signature on some systems in that biome. Stationary, alpha-capped, softened — it deepens the sky without ever competing with the planets you're reading.

6Ambient traffic · naturals & unnaturals

Busier, stranger skies

Flybys drift through the background — naturals (space happening) and unnaturals (someone's out here). Today ~1 in 3 systems gets one, one at a time. Below: the five that exist, six new ones, and the frequency bump.
